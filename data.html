Short Analysis

From the data we've gathered and seen so far, it seems that there are definitely differences in how people communicate, both in terms of the vocabulary they use and in the mannerisms they communicate it (though this is trivially obvious from everyday experience). What is important, however, is that the majority of people agree that there are certain dialects and specific dictions that they prefer. Let's take a look at some of the more precise data that we've gathered on that.


Graphical Representation of Perception of social class by individuals of distinct dialects

Perception of Social Class

Participants were asked to guess 4 individuals' social classes based on hearing their 4 distinct dialects of english. These results were converted to values 1 (lowest) to 5 (highest) Participants were given the option cannot identify. We see a significant discrepancy between graphs, so we have evidence that people do associate dialects with social class.
